The Good room - In the end of the reading, the author mentions a cold, quiet day, which instantly makes me think of a cool fall day, where life is quiet and peaceful. Those are truly the times where I can best work, and engage with any material I need to create. Those are the days I wait all year to come and they always make me want to do anything but be on technology. Maybe draw, paint, go outside, read a book something other than being trapped inside looking at a screen, the good days. The days that remind me of childhood, before we were addicted to a computer.

Laurel Schwulst - I really liked how she compared a website to a puddle. Puddles are always fresh and growing, just like how websites will start out as an expirement, and grow into something new over time. Puddles will evaporate, and then form again, in the same spot as fresh water. Your website is always going to be changing and forming into new ideas, like a puddle.
